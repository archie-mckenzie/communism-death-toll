# Communism Death Toll

Over 100 million dead in various genocides, classicides, democides, politicides, mass deportations, reigns of revolutionary terror, engineered famines, etc.

- **Red Terror**, Russian SFSR, 1918-1922: 100,000 to 200,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Terror))
- **Decossackization**, USSR, 1919-1933: 10,000 to 500,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decossackization))
- **Hungarian Red Terror**, Hungarian Soviet Republic, 1919: 370-590 executed <span style={{"whiteSpace": "nowrap"}}>([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Red_Terror))</span>
- **Povolzhye famine**, Russian SFSR, 1921-1922: 5 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_famine_of_1921))
  - **Turkestan famine**, 1919–1922: 400,000–750,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazakh_famine_of_1919%E2%80%931922))
- **Dekulakization**, USSR, 1929-1933: 530,000 to 600,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dekulakization))
- **Gulags**, USSR, 1929-1953: 1.2-1.7 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulag))
- **Population transfer**, 1930-1952: 800,000–1,500,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_transfer_in_the_Soviet_Union))
  - **Deportation of Koreans**, 1937: 16,500-50,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_Koreans_in_the_Soviet_Union))
  - **Deportation of the Volga Germans**, 1941: 42,823-228,800 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volga_Germans#Deportation_of_the_Volga_Germans))
  - **Deportations from Lithuania**, 1941-1952: 28,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_deportations_from_Lithuania))
  - **Deportations from Estonia**, 1941-1951: unknown number of deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_deportations_from_Estonia))
  - **Deportation of the Karachays**, 1943: 13,100—19,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Karachays))
  - **Deportation of the Kalmyks**, 1943: 16,017–16,594 deaths, between 17 and 19 percent of their total population ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Kalmyks))
  - **Deportation of the Chechens and Ingush**, 1944: 123,000–200,000 deaths or between 1/4 and 1/3 of their total population ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Chechens_and_Ingush))
  - **Deportation of the Balkars**, 1944: 7,600 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Balkars))
  - **Deportation of the Crimean Tatars**, 1944: 34,000 to 110,000 deaths, between 18 and 46 percent of their total population ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Crimean_Tatars))
  - **Deportation of the Meskhetian Turks**, 1944: 12,589 to 50,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_the_Meskhetian_Turks))
- **Famine**, USSR, 1932–1933: 6.4-12.5 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_famine_of_1932%E2%80%931933))
  - **Goloshchyokin genocide**, USSR, 1931–1933: 1.5-2.3 million deaths or between 38 to 42 percent of all Kazakhs ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazakh_famine_of_1931%E2%80%931933))
  - **Ukraine Terror-Famine**, USSR, 1932-1933: 3.5 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holodomor)), Genocide? ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holodomor_genocide_question))
- **Great Terror**, USSR, 1936-1938: between 950,000 and 1.2 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Purge))
  - **Mass operations of the NKVD** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_operations_of_the_NKVD))
    - **Repression of Anti-Soviet elements**, 1937-1938: 386,798 executed, NKVD Order #00447 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NKVD_Order_No._00447))
    - **Polish Operation**, 1937-1938: 111,091 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_Operation_of_the_NKVD)), NKVD Order #00485 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NKVD_Order_No._00485))
    - **Latvian Operation**, 1937-1938: at least 16,573 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latvian_Operation_of_the_NKVD))
    - **German Operation**, 1937-1938: 41,898 deaths, NKVD Order #00439 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NKVD_Order_No._00439))
    - **Harbin Operation**, 1937: 30,992 deaths, NKVD Order #00593 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NKVD_Order_No._00593))
    - **Greek Operation**, 1937-1950: 15,000-50,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Operation_of_the_NKVD))
  - **Repressions in Mongolia**, Mongolian People's Republic, 1937-1939: 20,000-35,000 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stalinist_repressions_in_Mongolia))
- **Spanish Red Terror**, 1936: 38,000 to 72,344 killed including 6,832 Roman Catholic Priests ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Terror_(Spain)))
- **Repression of Polish citizens**, USSR, 1939-1946: 150,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_repressions_of_Polish_citizens_(1939%E2%80%931946)))
- **Katyn massacre**, USSR, 1940: 22,000 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katyn_massacre))
- **Leftist Errors (Yugoslav Red Terror)**, Eventual Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, 1941: 1000+ deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leftist_errors))
- **Purges in Serbia**, Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, 1944-1945: at least 55,973 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_purges_in_Serbia_in_1944%E2%80%9345))
- **Forced labor of Hungarians**, USSR, 1944-1955: 200,000 perished ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forced_labor_of_Hungarians_in_the_Soviet_Union))
- **Socialist Republic of Romania**, 1945-1989: between 500,000 and two million deaths ([BBC](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/6190931.stm))
- **Augustów roundup**, Polish People's Republic, 1945: 2000 executed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August%C3%B3w_roundup))
- **Land Reform Movement**, People’s Republic of China, 1946-1953: 200,000 – 5,000,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_Reform_Movement_(China)))
- **Famine**, USSR, 1946-1947: 500,000 to 2 million ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_famine_of_1946%E2%80%931947))
- **Campaign to Suppress Counterrevolutionaries**, PRC, 1950-1953: 1-2 million executed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campaign_to_Suppress_Counterrevolutionaries))
- **Land Reform**, Democratic Republic of Vietnam, 1953-1956: 15,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_reform_in_North_Vietnam))
- **Hungarian Uprising**, Hungarian People's Republic, 1956: ~3000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1956))
- **Tibetan uprising**, PRC, 1959: 85,000-87,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1959_Tibetan_uprising))
- **Great Chinese Famine**, PRC, 1959-1961: 15-55 million deaths, making it the largest famine in human history ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Chinese_Famine))
- **Cultural Revolution**, PRC, 1966-1976: hundreds of thousands to 20 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_Revolution))
  - **Red August**, 1966: 10,000+ according to official CCP 1985 statistics, massacred in and around Beijing by the Red Guard ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_August)), including the Daxing Massacre where 325 were killed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daxing_Massacre))
  - **Violent Struggle**, 1966-1968: 300,000-500,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Violent_Struggle))
  - **Guangxi Massacre**, 1967-1976: 100,000-150,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangxi_Massacre))
  - **Inner Mongolia incident**, 1967-1969: 20,000-100,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_Mongolia_incident))
  - **Cleansing the Class Ranks**, 1968: 0.5-1.5 million deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleansing_the_Class_Ranks))
- **Banqiao Dam failure**, PRC, 1975: 85,600 to 240,000 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1975_Banqiao_Dam_failure))
- **Cambodian Genocide**, Democratic Kampuchea, 1975-1979: 1.5-2 million deaths or a quarter of the population ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodian_genocide))
  - **Prison 21**: 18,133 killed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuol_Sleng_Genocide_Museum))
  - **Killing Fields**: 1.7-2.5 million killed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Killing_Fields))
- **Qey Shibir (Ethiopian Red Terror)**, Provisional Military Government of Socialist Ethiopia or the Derg, 1976-1977: 30,000 to 750,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Terror_(Ethiopia)))
- **Pul-e-Charkhi prison**, Democratic Republic of Afghanistan, 1978-1979: 27,000 political prisoners executed ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pul-e-Charkhi_prison))
- **Soviet–Afghan War**, Democratic Republic of Afghanistan, 1979-1989: 562,000 to 2,000,000 deaths or 6.5%–11.5% of the population ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet%E2%80%93Afghan_War))
- **Ethiopian Famine**, Provisional Military Government of Socialist Ethiopia or the Derg, 1983-1985: 200,000–1,200,000 deaths ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1983%E2%80%931985_famine_in_Ethiopia))
